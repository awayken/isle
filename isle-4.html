Everyone sat where they could, or had laid down, and had begun making that same low frequency singing and giggling. I watched as smiles stayed firmly upon their faces. People were actually smiling.

It had been years since I had smiled. I wanted to smile very badly at that moment. I wanted to more than anything. I placed the pill in my mouth. I heard a sound, a ripping. I swallowed. The sound was coming from one of the people in the lobby. She started singing louder and louder; her mouth was trying to open wider to sing, but her jaw was clenched tight.

Suddenly she screamed and arched her back. She threw her arms out at the sides, hitting some people next to her, and I saw her make tight fists at the end. Then, in a quick movement, she brought her hands to her stomach, dug her fingers into the skin, and pulled. The skin tore up to her solar plexis and down to below her belt, and she relaxed as if she had released all that threatened to tear her before. There was no blood, but steam started to rise from the hole in her body. From where I stood I couldn't make out any internal organs or bones or muscles, but I could hear her heart.
