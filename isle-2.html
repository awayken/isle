always found myself in that Isle. "Isle" is the word that the grocery manager used to replace "Aisle" because he thought it was clever. They called this one "Paradise Isle". I guess it was a running joke or a cute campaign to sell the product. It worked on the majority, on the flock, but I had never purchased one. The products they sold in Paradise Isle looked just like the other products in the grocery store. Some were cans, some were boxes, some were abnormally shaped packages. You could look at them and say, "That would be Mountain Dew" or "That is definately Tide."

The common theme betwixt all the products was that they had no label. There was no ingredient list, no trademark, no name. There was only a price, sometimes in a starburst in the corner, and the decorative colors.

Everyone loved this Isle. My friends would rave about it. The products changed their lives. The products made them feel complete and whole. The products were like paradise. This they said with funny, stiff grins and unblinking and dry eyes. These people had turned into TV characters waiting for the canned audience response before they continued.

Still, it was intriguing. There was no ad campaign at all. Suddenly, in the center of the store, there was an Isle made up entirely of unmarked products. At first, I'm sure, people thought they were marked down versions of the real products. I'm sure that most of the patrons thought these were rejects and that they were getting a deal. That is probably why they thought these things were so great to begin with. They expected the sinner, but they bought the Savior.
