had seen what the Savior looked like. I was at a friend's house, dining, and after the meal he pulled out an unmarked Pepsi can. He opened in and then held it with the mouth down and the bottom of the can up. No liquid came out, instead a tiny pill landed on the table top. He took the pill in his palm, slapped the palm to his mouth, tilted his head back, and swallowed it down. He closed his eyes for a long ten seconds and then opened them again.

He offered me one that night. I took it and didn't open it. It had the same weight as a can of soda. You could hear the soda inside, if you shook it, and the can perspired as if there were liquid inside. I soon departed, thanking my friend for the gift, but complaining of being very tired. I'm not sure that he heard me. He had begun to sing at a very, very low frequency and to giggle from time to time while watching his wall clock. He didn't notice me leave.

Now here I was in the Isle again. Staring at the products that everyone buys. Some people don't even buy food anymore. They walk straight to Paradise Isle, take the closest product, and pay for it on their way out. These are people who haven't seen a thought since the day they discovered Paradise. These are people whose families have broken over this pill. Then the family starts to use, and they aren't broken anymore. Addicts. Losers.
